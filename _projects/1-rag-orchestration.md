This project studies collaborative cloud-edge scheduling for retrieval-augmented generation services. The selected execution node affects not only computing and communication costs, but also the external knowledge available to the task.

- Jointly model task semantics, knowledge-database coverage, computing cost, transmission delay, and energy consumption.
- Design knowledge-matching-aware task orchestration under dynamic loads and heterogeneous resources.
- Build a simulation environment for multi-node collaborative RAG inference.
- Achieve approximately 50% lower energy consumption and more than 10% lower service delay in experiments.
