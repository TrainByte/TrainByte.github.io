This project studies real-time wireless resource scheduling that improves long-term fairness while preserving overall network service performance.

## Research problem

How can wireless networks make real-time scheduling decisions under imperfect channel-state information while optimizing long-term fairness?

## My role

Lead researcher and first author.

## Methods

- Imperfect CSI modeling
- Long-term alpha-fair utility
- Adaptive reward design
- Multi-agent deep reinforcement learning

## Main results

The proposed method improved long-term fairness by more than 13% while increasing the overall service rate by more than 10%.
