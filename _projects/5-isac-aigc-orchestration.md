## Overview

This project studies AIGC service orchestration under integrated sensing and communication. The work links environmental sensing accuracy, generated content quality, communication rate, computing resources, and user service experience into one optimization framework.

## Challenge

- AIGC service quality depends on sensing accuracy, generation quality, communication transmission, and computing allocation at the same time.
- The resource-allocation problem contains tightly coupled sensing, communication, and computing decisions.
- Learning-based scheduling needs structured optimization to remain stable and efficient.

## My Contributions

- Served as a main contributor and first author.
- Designed a content-accuracy-and-quality-aware service metric.
- Built a multi-user AIGC service model connecting sensing, computing, communication, and user experience.
- Formulated the joint resource-allocation problem.
- Designed an LP-guided hierarchical reinforcement learning framework: the upper level optimizes sensing and computing resources, while the lower level uses linear programming for communication resource allocation.
- Completed comparative simulations, ablation studies, and performance analysis.

## Outcomes

The proposed method improved average user service experience by more than 50% in the evaluated scenarios. The work was published in IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing.
