## Overview

Built an ISAC-driven AIGC service orchestration framework that evaluates and optimizes content accuracy, content quality, sensing accuracy, computing resources, and communication resources as a coupled service system.

## Challenge

- Existing AIGC service metrics often focus on generation quality while underrepresenting content accuracy and sensing-dependent service context.
- Sensing, computing, and communication resources interact with one another and jointly affect AIGC service quality.
- The resource-allocation problem requires both learning-based decision making and structured optimization.

## My Contributions

- Identified the service-assessment limitation in generation-quality-only evaluation.
- Designed the content-accuracy-and-quality-aware metric.
- Modeled sensing-computing-communication coupling in ISAC-driven AIGC services.
- Formulated the joint resource-allocation problem.
- Designed an LP-guided hierarchical deep reinforcement learning framework.
- Implemented comparative simulations.
- Performed ablation and performance analysis.

## Technical Highlights

- CAQA service assessment
- Sensing-computing-communication resource coupling
- LP-guided hierarchical DRL
- Ablation and comparative performance analysis

## Outcomes

- Improved the content-accuracy-and-quality-aware metric by more than 10% compared with learning baselines.
- Improved the metric by more than 50% compared with schemes considering generation quality alone.
