## Overview

This project studies cloud-edge collaborative scheduling for multi-user AIGC services. The work addresses dynamic user requests, heavy model inference cost, and limited edge resources through a distributed hybrid quantum-classical reinforcement learning framework.

## Challenge

- AIGC tasks create heavy computing demand and strict delay requirements.
- User requests arrive dynamically, while edge nodes have limited computing resources.
- Classical policy networks can become large and expensive to train for complex scheduling states.

## My Contributions

- Served as a main contributor for modeling and algorithm design.
- Built a cloud-edge collaborative processing model coupling task offloading, computing resource allocation, and service delay.
- Designed a distributed hybrid quantum-classical reinforcement learning framework.
- Used parameterized quantum circuits to enhance policy representation while combining classical neural networks for collaborative decision making.
- Evaluated scheduling delay, parameter scale, and training complexity against baseline methods.

## Outcomes

Compared with baseline scheduling methods, the proposed framework reduced model parameters by more than 95% while reducing average AIGC service delay by more than 30% in the evaluated scenarios. The related work has been submitted to a CCF-A journal.
