## Overview

This project focuses on adaptive fair resource scheduling in multi-cell multi-user downlink communication scenarios with imperfect channel-state information. The work studies how channel estimation errors and inter-cell resource competition affect long-term fairness and network throughput.

## Challenge

- Wireless scheduling decisions are sensitive to channel uncertainty and estimation errors.
- Long-term fairness and instantaneous service rate often pull decisions in different directions.
- Multi-cell resource competition creates coupled decisions across users and cells.

## My Contributions

- Served as a main contributor and first author.
- Built an uncertainty-aware robust resource allocation model for imperfect channel-state information.
- Designed an adaptive fairness mechanism driven by multi-agent deep reinforcement learning.
- Dynamically adjusted reward functions and scheduling policies to balance long-term fairness with network service rate.
- Completed simulations, ablation analysis, and paper writing.

## Outcomes

The proposed method improved the long-term fairness index by 13% while increasing network service rate by more than 10% in the evaluated scenarios. The work was published in IEEE Transactions on Cognitive Communications and Networking, a CAS Q1 TOP journal.
